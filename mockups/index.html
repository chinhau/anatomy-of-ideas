Three ways to cross the threshold

Each card opens a self-contained, animated mockup of one signature landing moment. Press the enter button or ↻ Replay inside each to re-watch. All share the same engraved spine and struck-gold technique — only the metaphor changes. The live decision is B vs D — abstract truth (a self-ruling star-chart) against figurative warmth (a blooming lotus with human seekers inside it). Open both; tell me which one makes a stranger lean in.

Direction B

The Dark Sky-Atlas

A graticule rules itself; the eleven questions ignite as struck stars, sized by influence, and resolve into one constellation under the pole star.

Why it wins: brightness = a real datum (each question's weight); the atlas is already a star-chart. Highest screenshot value.
Cost: ~0 bytes baseline.

Direction D

The Living Cosmogram

A lone seeker looks up; the moon hangs true tonight; the golden flower of eleven questions blooms open around them as every seeker joins, the heart struck once in gold.

Why it wins: the figurative warmth B refuses — humans in the scene, a sacred bloom. The emotional, "you are standing inside it" register.

Direction A

The Living Codex

The void is a shut book. The clasp-seal engraves, the gold gutter-seam brightens, and the cover swings open to the frontispiece.

Why it wins: literally what the product is — a bound book; cleanest tab-to-tab "page-turn" grammar.

Direction C

The Alchemist's Great Work

Formless prime materia calcines inward into the seal; a raking band of light strikes it from base brown to struck gold as the title coheres.

Why it wins: names the alchemy already in the seal; gives one motion law — solve et coagula — for the whole site.
