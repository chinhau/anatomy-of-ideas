The same ego-graph, two ways

Focus: Anattā (No-Self), c.250 BCE. Its neighbours are the same in both views — only their angular position changes. Today the ring spaces them evenly by list order (position means nothing). The era-clock keys each neighbour’s angle to its own historical date, so the dial reads like a clock of time — and the two echoes visibly leap to the far, modern side of the dial, two millennia from the focus’s contemporaries.

builds on rejects echoes (independent rhyme)

Why this serves the atom: the atlas’s thesis is independent convergence — the same move reached by cultures that never met. In the even ring you cannot see that. In the era-clock, an echo sitting 2,000 years around the dial from the focus is the convergence, drawn to scale. The change is small and low-risk: only the angle function changes (index → era); nodes, edges, labels and interactions are untouched.
