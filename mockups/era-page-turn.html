Opening a dossier as a turning page

The dossier turns in like a page of a codex. “Era-keyed” means the only datum it can carry is the idea’s date: older ideas turn a touch slower and wear a warmer, sepia paper; modern ideas snap in quicker and cooler. Toggle the exaggeration to see how faint that signal really is at honest strength.

Pick a concept to open its dossier →

Honest read: the turn is handsome, but the only thing it encodes is the era, which the dossier already states in words. At truthful strength the keying is nearly invisible; turned up enough to notice, it risks feeling like a gimmick. This is the item flagged as ornament rather than data.

By the project’s own rule — “every flourish must encode a real datum” — this one is the weakest of Phase 2. Worth seeing before deciding whether it earns a place.
