# The Anatomy of Ideas — Mission

**Status:** Draft for review · **Date:** 2026-06-23
**How this was made:** Four independent agent critiques (epistemics, product
strategy, editorial/scope, Jobs-simplicity) were run against the maintainer's
raw framing and converged on the reframing below.

---

## The one sentence

> **Every question you think is yours, humans have kept asking for 2,500 years —
> come see where it stands now.**

The feeling it should leave: *you're not the first to wonder.* Not awe at how
much we know — relief that the 3 a.m. thought puts you inside a long
conversation, standing at a real frontier rather than alone in the dark.

## North-star mission

This atlas exists to show, **honestly**, where human thought actually stands on
the great questions we keep asking — what has quietly settled, what
we've only *sharpened*, and what stays genuinely open. It maps 289 ideas across
eleven such questions and 2,500 years for the curious non-specialist (and the
philosophy-literate skeptic), so a reader leaves seeing the inquiry as a
navigable landscape with real frontiers — not a pile of opinions, and not a
fairy tale of "answers found." The change it makes: the reader stops asking
*"who said what?"* and starts asking *"where are we now, and what's next?"*

**Vision:** a world where anyone curious can see, at a glance, the whole arc of
humanity's hardest questions — where progress is real and where humility is
required — and feels invited to stand at the frontier rather than spectate.

## Scope: not "metaphysics," but the great questions

The raw mission said "metaphysically." We are deliberately **not** using that
word as the cover term. The project plainly spans ethics (*How should I live?*),
politics (*What is a just society?*), mind, and meaning; "metaphysics" would
either misdescribe the page or force a scope-cut that guts the most-visited
questions. The honest superset is the one the atlas already enacts:

> **the great questions humans keep asking.**

"Metaphysical" is kept only for the *local* sense (*What is real?*), never as the
name of the whole territory.

## The epistemic spine — what "progress" honestly means

Metaphysics almost never delivers "X is true, Y is false." It delivers **change
in the *status* of a question.** So we replace the binary "proven true/untrue"
with five honest states, and we say where each of the eleven mostly sits (most
questions straddle more than one):

1. **Handed off to science** — the question kept its name but its centre of
   gravity moved to an empirical discipline that now constrains it. The
   philosophy didn't lose; it *spawned* the science.
   → *What is real?* (cosmology/physics), *How does the mind work?* (neuroscience).
2. **Hardened consensus (within a frame)** — no experiment closed it, but
   competent inquirers have effectively stopped relitigating it. Some
   sub-questions are genuinely *closed* (Gödel, Tarski).
   → parts of *What can I know?*
3. **Live rival positions** — several mature, articulate camps persist; the
   disagreement is stable and informed, not confusion.
   → *What is a just society?*, *How should I live?*
4. **Perennially open** — resists resolution in principle, usually because of an
   evaluative or first-person residue science can't absorb.
   → *Is the will free?*, *Is there an Absolute?*, *What makes life worth
   living?*, the hard problem inside *the mind* / *the self*.
5. **Dissolved / reframed** — the original question rested on a bad
   presupposition; retired or rewritten rather than answered.
   → much of *What is the self?* (Humean/Buddhist/Parfitian deflation),
   *Why do we suffer?* read metaphysically.

**Two kinds of change, only one is progress.** Genuine progress = movement along
this list (handed off, closed, dissolved). *Fashion* = a position cycling back
into favour with no new constraint — express that as **recurrence**, not advance
(the Timeline's per-question lanes already do exactly this). "Boundaries pushed"
therefore means **new constraints we cannot un-know** — not "we now believe Z."
Progress is *shrinking the space of tenable positions*, never *arriving at the
true one*.

## Decision principles (apply to every change — an idea, a feature, a sentence)

1. **Map the inquiry, not the canon.** Include an idea only if it changes the
   shape of a question — a new position, boundary, or refutation. "Famous
   thinker, no distinct move" is a cut.
2. **The honesty bar is a veto, not a vibe.** If a claim can't be stated without
   overclaiming, flag it (`contested`) or cut it. Draw only what the data
   supports — discrete dots, never interpolated lines; "recurrent," never
   "perennial," unless every era-bucket backs it. A feature that *implies* more
   certainty than we have fails this test even if it's beautiful.
3. **Show the verdict, not just the voices.** Prefer features that locate where
   we *are* (settled / contested / open) over features that merely show more
   content.
4. **Even-handed by construction.** No question, tradition, or region wins by
   volume. When one badge or lane dominates the dataset, that imbalance is a bug
   to fix, not a flavour to keep.
5. **One feeling per view.** Each view delivers a single legible takeaway. If a
   second encoding competes with the primary one, cut the weaker.
6. **Idea-centric beats name-centric.** Foreground the move, let the thinker
   follow. "Double Consciousness" before "Du Bois."
7. **Self-contained and free.** Defend the single-file, no-backend, no-paywall
   form. Any dependency or gate must justify itself against open, frictionless
   access.

## The test

> For any proposed change, ask: **"Does this help the reader see where human
> thought *actually is* on a great question, honestly stated? If not, cut it."**

## What the honesty bar forbids (in our own framing)

- **"proven" / "solved" / "proven true or untrue"** — imports a verificationist
  standard metaphysics doesn't meet; would tempt overclaiming on *free will*,
  *the Absolute*, *meaning*. Use *settled / sharpened / open*.
- **"common consensus"** stated flatly — hedge to *"positions that have quietly
  hardened into near-consensus,"* and reserve a distinct register for *negative*
  consensus (what we've ruled out).
- **"boundaries we've pushed"** as self-congratulation — pair exploration with
  *"how much is still unmapped."*
- Treating **"handed off to science"** as **"answered"** — handing off relocates
  a question; the perennial residue survives all the neuroscience.
